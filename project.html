I BELIEVE IN

EAT (); SLEEP (); CODE (); REPEAT ();

ProGrad

I am DurrayNayab (try pronouncing, I know it’s difficult). I am doing my graduation. To be precise I am a so-called B-Tech student who is not clear with the whole idea called ‘life’. You must thinking that I am yet another kid who is studying a degree course under parents’ pressure. However, I am not. My parents are more supportive than anyone can imagine. They wanted me to decide for myself. I however have a huge number of passions to pursue but I settled for a tech course to be on the safe side. Safe side, you see have a twisted meaning on its own, changing from person to person. Some seek refuge in fighting for something while others find peace in death. And since most of us work towards decreasing the entropy, we tend to settle for a “proved safe side”. We never glance towards the “road not taken”. So, hell yeah I took a safe side. My parents did not object while giving me the power to decide my fate. I was not sure and probably flawed my decision. But now when I connect the dots backwards I realize what my parents did for me. They taught me a lesson for life that how important it is to take decisions on your own and what is the importance of learning to take your decisions. Not everyone has the courage to take their own decision. But if you see deep into it, you will realize how being able to make decisions make a difference in your life. If not anything else at least, you will tend to have less regrets. I do not say that I have zero regrets but I cannot possibly hold someone else responsible for my own regrets. It was my doings, circumstantial or not, which has led to where I am today and clearly, it were my decisions. With each decision I made carved my path till this point. Now when look back and try to imagine about things if I would have decided differently, one thing that comes clear is, it wouldn’t have been better. I believe there is something called fate but also believes all humans have the power to change the fate. That’s how decisions come into picture. I am not the correct person to speak about this particular topic. But, India gives us all the freedom of speech. So why waste it??? There are two different kind of decision one can make. One, with all the research and statistics and second, with absolute recklessness. The former is type is absolutely stereotype. You think about something for days, research about it and if the fog clears, you take a decision. But if you ask my personal opinion, go with the latter. That will save you time and the frustration of getting confused. Also, when you are deciding this way you will want to gather all your powers to make your decision prove right. And the loophole in the former type is after doing all the research, you will be so sure of yourself that you wouldn’t want to shift a pebble to catalyze the process (of whatever you are trying to do). Now you must be thinking “how on earth this lady is lecturing us on decision making when she, most probably made the wrong one?” . I understand! you can hate me for that. But let’s just say that even I am working hard to prove my decision correct. Prove it to myself that I am not the kind of engineer that is portrayed by facebook. That happens when you make your own decision. When you make your own decision, you want to prove yourself right and work hard for it. And when you don’t get a chance of making your own decision, you want to prove it wrong (maybe, unintentionally). I think your decisions make you a better person because working hard to get something is never really wrong. And I am proud of my parents that they made me learn to take a stand for myself and prove my own individuality in a crowd full of stereotypes. P. S: I don’t know why I named it “Twisted Thoughts”. Probably because I did not realize my parents contributed so much more in my life beyond the materialistic help. It all occurred to me during the course of writing this piece. So yeah, it was twisted before understanding creep in.
